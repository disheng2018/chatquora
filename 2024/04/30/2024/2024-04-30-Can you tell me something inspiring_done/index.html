1. English

Can you tell me something inspiring?

Akshay

​ 134.8K views · 14.0k Upvotes

There is a juice stall, in proximity of my house. The owner is a hard-working guy, who hails from a humble background in Bihar. Someone, who has seen poverty from close quarters. I talk to him every day.

A day before yesterday, I could not.

‘He might be unwell or something’, I thought.

Yesterday, he did arrive. Surprisingly, the stall was replaced with a single table. And, ‘mango juice’ was the sole item he sold.


“What happened?” I asked.

“They [corporation] took the stall,” he said.

“Oh, now?”

“Now, what? I have this,” he tapped the table,” Does it look any less beautiful than the stall?”

I was awestruck.

The man was snatched of his living, a day before. And, here he was, smiling. Fighting, with whatever he had. Call it a necessity, if you may. Blame it on destiny, if you want.

For me, it’s a lesson.

Of battling. Of failing. Of taking the blows and yet grinding it out with the chin up. Of hope and helplessness.

Of life, and so much more.

2. 中英译文

你能给我讲一些鼓舞人心的事儿吗？_Quora文选

There is a juice stall, in proximity of my house. The owner is a hard-working guy, who hails from a humble background in Bihar. Someone, who has seen poverty from close quarters. I talk to him every day.
我家附近有个果汁摊位，老板是个勤劳的人，来自比哈尔一个朴素的家庭。他是那种亲眼目睹过贫穷的人。我每天都会和他聊天。

A day before yesterday, I could not.
前天，我没看到他。

‘He might be unwell or something’, I thought.
‘他可能生病了或者有什么事儿’，我想。

Yesterday, he did arrive. Surprisingly, the stall was replaced with a single table. And, ‘mango juice’ was the sole item he sold.
昨天，他确实来了。让人惊讶的是，摊位被一张桌子取代了。而且，‘芒果汁’成了他唯一卖的东西。


“What happened?” I asked.
“怎么回事？” 我问道。

“They [corporation] took the stall,” he said.
“他们[公司]拿走了摊位。” 他说道。

“Oh, now?”
“哦，现在呢？”

“Now, what? I have this,” he tapped the table,” Does it look any less beautiful than the stall?”
“现在怎样？我有这个。” 他轻拍了拍桌子，“它看起来比摊位差吗？”

I was awestruck.
我被震撼到了。

The man was snatched of his living, a day before. And, here he was, smiling. Fighting, with whatever he had. Call it a necessity, if you may. Blame it on destiny, if you want.
就在前一日，这名男士失去了谋生之道。然而，在此刻的他依旧面带微笑，用手中的所剩无几继续奋战着。如果你愿意可以称之为必要性；如果你喜欢也可以把这个归咎于命运。

For me, it’s a lesson.
对于我而言，这是一个教训。

Of battling. Of failing. Of taking the blows and yet grinding it out with the chin up. Of hope and helplessness.
关于战斗、失败、承受打击同时仍挺胸抬头向前行。关于希望与无助。

Of life, and so much more.
关于生活及其更多层面。
