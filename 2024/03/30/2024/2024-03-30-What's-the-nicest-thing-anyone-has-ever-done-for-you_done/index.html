1. English

What’s the nicest thing anyone has ever done for you?

Bruno Valle · Works at Bain & Company

​ 231.6K views · 14.5k Upvotes

I was on a train in France in 2006 - the overnight train from Nice to Rome.

Being young backpackers, my friend and I had paid for the cheapest fare possible, which meant sleeping in tight chairs in a packed car. At some point in the middle of the night, I get woken up by a rough hand shaking my shoulder.


I looked up to see a tall blonde man in a baseball cap looking down at me.

“Someone just took something out of your bag!”, he said to me in an American accent, before waking up a few people next to us which had also been robbed.

Once we awakened, he explained “I just saw three guys rummaging through your bags and taking out cash and valuables. When they saw me they went straight to back of the train, we need to get them!”

I didn’t think it was safe to confront 3 train robbers in the middle of the night in the last car of a speeding train, so I said “Shouldn’t we tell the conductor first?” The baseball cap guy was seething and clearly didn’t want to wait for the conductor. He said “Ok, you go to the front and tell the conductor, and…” he looked around, trying to find someone or something.

His gaze landed on a hulking curled up figure, sleeping under a blanket and cap. This sleeping guy was at least 6’6’’.

Baseball cap guy walked towards him shook him roughly “You, hey, wake up, I need your help. Three guys just ran down the train car stealing from bags, we need to get them.”

The sleeping guy pulled off his blanket and stood up. He was gigantic, and was wearing an “Australia Rugby” shirt.

The baseball guy was clearly satisfied with his new recruit, and they marched to the back of the train followed by the excited group of victims and onlookers. I made my way to the front of the train and alerted the conductor of what was happening – he said he couldn’t do anything until we reached Marseille, when he would ask the “railroad police” to board the train and investigate the thefts.

Dejected, I went back to my seat to tell the other travelers about this, but no one was there. I could hear yelling coming from the back of the train so I walked past the next few cars and got there. There was a commotion in front of the last door, as baseball cap guy, Australian rugby guy and 10 other people jostled and yelled at the last door.


The 3 suspects had been chased down by the baseball/rugby duo and had barricaded themselves in the last car. They were clearly scared, but were screaming back at the crowd in broken English “We have knife! We have knife!”.

Soon, baseball/rugby duo decided they had enough. The massive Australian rugby player started to kick the door repeatedly, putting his full weight behind each kick. The cutpurses stared at the door after each kick, trying to see how long it could take them. They talked among themselves and made a decision.

The trio pulled out thousands of Euros in cash, jewelry and other valuables and dropped them on the ground, saying “Ok! Ok! What you want!” and ran to the back of the last car.

Soon team baseball/rugby had gotten the door open, but the 3 hoodlums were gone – they had jumped out of the moving train in fear.

But baseball cap guy and rugby guy were celebrated as heroes for the rest of the ride. If they hadn’t intervened, the robbers would have escaped with the money when the train slowed down. The baseball cap guy was a retired cop from New York, and the rugby guy just played rugby in university.

I think they both deserve “nicest thing anyone has ever done for me.”

别人对你做过最美的事情是什么？_Quora文选

I was on a train in France in 2006 - the overnight train from Nice to Rome.
2006年，我在法国乘坐了一列从尼斯开往罗马的夜班火车。

Being young backpackers, my friend and I had paid for the cheapest fare possible, which meant sleeping in tight chairs in a packed car. At some point in the middle of the night, I get woken up by a rough hand shaking my shoulder.
作为年轻的背包客，我和我的朋友选择了最便宜的票价，这意味着我们得在拥挤的车厢里紧靠着椅子睡觉。半夜时分，一个粗糙的手摇晃我的肩膀，将我唤醒。

I looked up to see a tall blonde man in a baseball cap looking down at me.
我抬头看到一个戴棒球帽、金发碧眼的高个男子正俯视着我。

“Someone just took something out of your bag!”, he said to me in an American accent, before waking up a few people next to us which had also been robbed.
“有人刚从你包里拿走东西！”他用美国口音对我说，并叫醒了我们旁边几位也遭窃的人。

Once we awakened, he explained “I just saw three guys rummaging through your bags and taking out cash and valuables. When they saw me they went straight to back of the train, we need to get them!”
我们醒来后，他解释道：“我刚看到三个家伙在你们包里翻找并取出现金和贵重物品。当他们看到我时就直奔火车尾部，我们得去抓住他们！”

I didn’t think it was safe to confront 3 train robbers in the middle of the night in the last car of a speeding train, so I said “Shouldn’t we tell the conductor first?” The baseball cap guy was seething and clearly didn’t want to wait for the conductor. He said “Ok, you go to the front and tell the conductor, and…” he looked around, trying to find someone or something.
面对三名火车强盗，在深夜飞驰中的列车末节车厢内与之正面冲突，在我看来并不安全，因此我提议：“我们是不是应该先告诉列车员？”那位戴棒球帽的男士怒气冲冲，并显然不想等待列车员处理。“好吧，你去前头告诉列车员。”他环顾四周寻找某人或某物时说道。

His gaze landed on a hulking curled up figure, sleeping under a blanket and cap. This sleeping guy was at least 6’6’’.
他目光落在一个裹在毛毯下、戴着帽子沉睡中巨大身影上，这个沉睡者至少有六英尺六英寸高。

Baseball cap guy walked towards him shook him roughly “You, hey wake up! I need your help. Three guys just ran down this way stealing from bags; we need catch them.”
棒球帽男士走向他并粗暴地摇晃，“喂！快醒醒！需要你帮忙，刚才有三个家伙往这边跑偷东西了，咱们得追上他们。”

The sleeping guy pulled off his blanket and stood up. He was gigantic ,and wearing an “Australia Rugby” shirt .
那沉睡者掀开毛毯站起身来，体型庞大且穿着一件“澳洲橄榄球”衬衫 。

The baseball guy clearly satisfied with his new recruit ,and they marched toward back of train followed by excited group victims onlookers .I made my way front alerting conductor what happening –he said couldn’t do anything until reached Marseille when would ask “railroad police” board investigate thefts .
棒球帽男士显然对这新招募的人很满意，随即领队向火车后部进发，被劫及围观群众跟随其后。我则前往通知司机所发生之事 —— 司机表示除非达到马赛否则无能为力，届时会请求铁路警方登船调查失窃事件。

Dejected ,went back seat telling other travelers about this but no one there .could hear yelling coming back so past next few cars got there commotion front last door as baseball cap Australian rugby others jostled yelled last door .
我心情沮丧地回到座位欲告知其他旅客此事，但无人在场。能听见后方传来喊声于是越过数节 车厢抵达现场 在最后一扇门前 棍球帽与澳洲橄榄球衬衣以及其他十余人推挤喊叫 。


The 3 suspects had been chased down by the baseball/rugby duo and had barricaded themselves in the last car. They were clearly scared, but were screaming back at the crowd in broken English “We have knife! We have knife!”.
三名嫌疑人被棒球/橄榄球二人组追赶，并在最后一节车厢内设下了防线。他们显然很害怕，但仍用蹩脚的英语对着人群大喊：“我们有刀！我们有刀！”

Soon, baseball/rugby duo decided they had enough. The massive Australian rugby player started to kick the door repeatedly, putting his full weight behind each kick. The cutpurses stared at the door after each kick, trying to see how long it could take them. They talked among themselves and made a decision.
不久，棒球/橄榄球二人组决定采取行动。那位体型庞大的澳洲橄榄球运动员开始反复踢门，每一次都倾尽全力。扒手们每次踢门后都盯着门看，试图估算自己还能撑多久。他们相互讨论并做出了决定。

The trio pulled out thousands of Euros in cash, jewelry and other valuables and dropped them on the ground, saying “Ok! Ok! What you want!” and ran to the back of the last car.
这三个人拿出数千欧元现金、珠宝和其他贵重物品丢在地上，说道：“Ok！Ok！给你们想要的！”然后跑到了最后一节车厢的末端。

Soon team baseball/rugby had gotten the door open, but the 3 hoodlums were gone – they had jumped out of the moving train in fear.
不久之后，棒球/橄榄球二人组打开了门，但是那三个嫌疑人已经消失了 —— 他们因为恐惧而从移动中的火车上跳了下去。

But baseball cap guy and rugby guy were celebrated as heroes for the rest of the ride. If they hadn’t intervened, the robbers would have escaped with money when train slowed down. The baseball cap guy was a retired cop from New York, and rugby guy just played rugby in university.
但是戴棒球帽的哥们儿和玩橄榄球的哥们儿在剩余旅程中被当作英雄来庆贺，如果他们没有介入，当火车减速时强盗就会带着钱逃跑了。戴棒球帽的哥们儿是纽约退役警察，而玩橄榄球的哥们儿只是在大学里玩过橄榄球罢了。

I think they both deserve “nicest thing anyone has ever done for me.”
我认为他们俩都值得“任何人为我所做过最美好事情”的称号。
